# Overview

The **ARCHY layer** represents the planetary simulation and system
modeling environment within the NEXAH framework.

ARCHY explores the dynamics of complex planetary systems including:

• climate stress  
• water systems  
• food production  
• energy systems  
• financial contagion  
• geopolitical conflict  
• migration flows  
• infrastructure networks  
• global urban evolution  

The layer enables the construction of **integrated world system
simulations** capable of exploring stability, resilience, and collapse
scenarios.

---

The module set has expanded significantly during recent development and

# Architectural Position in the Framework

The NEXAH framework currently follows the layered structure:

META — conceptual research layer
↓
NEXAH — navigation & policy logic
↓
ARCHY — planetary simulation layer
↓
MESO — stability & collapse analysis
↓
MEVA — execution engine
↓
core — system schema & loaders


ARCHY acts as the **world simulation layer** connecting theoretical
concepts to executable system models.

---

# Current Limitations

ARCHY currently remains an **experimental research sandbox**.

Known limitations include:

• simplified climate dynamics  
• simplified economic models  
• limited calibration with real-world data  
• simplified demographic models  
• absence of energy transition modeling  
• limited policy modeling capabilities  

These simplifications are intentional and allow rapid exploration of
system structures.

---

# Development Philosophy

ARCHY is designed as an **exploratory modeling environment** rather than
a fully calibrated Earth system model.

The goal is to explore:

• structural fragility  
• cascade dynamics  
• collapse pathways  
• resilience architectures  

This approach allows rapid experimentation with complex system models.
